The tasks of this user study consist of rewriting given sentences from abstracts of scientific publications. You should rewrite them as one or more sentences of a form that we call "AIDA sentences." An AIDA sentence is named like that, because it is Atomic, Independent, Declarative and Absolute: Atomic: a sentence describing one thought that cannot be further broken down (in a practical way) Independent: a sentence that can stand on its own, without external references like "this effect" or "we" Declarative: a complete sentence ending with a full stop that could (in theory) be either true or false Absolute: a sentence describing the core of a claim ignoring the (un)certainty about its truth (no "may," "data suggests that," or "significantly") and ignoring how it was discovered (no "evaluation showed that") The absoluteness criterion implies that AIDA sentences are typically in present tense. Below are two examples of how original sentences from biomedical abstracts can be rewritten as AIDA sentences. Note that in other cases such sentences may already be in AIDA format and no changes are necessary.

Example 1

Assume that the following original text is given: "The results of our study suggest that the MTHFR gene may influence the onset of menarche and natural menopause. This effect is probably due to the multiple SNP-SNP and SNP-environment interactions." In this case, you could come up with the following two AIDA sentences (other correct solutions are possible): AIDA sentence 1: The MTHFR gene influences the onset of menarche and natural menopause. AIDA sentence 2: The effect of the MTHFR gene on the onset of menarche and natural menopause is due to multiple SNP-SNP and SNP-environment interactions. Two notes on this example: Note 1: Things like "The results of our study suggest," "may" and "probably" are dropped for the sake of absolute sentences. Note 2: "This effect" of the second original sentence needs to be expanded to "The effect of the MTHFR gene on the onset of menarche and natural menopause" to make the sentence independent.

Example 2

The following original text is given: "Although we have found a slightly lower risk than other reports, the risk of developing neurodegenerative disease in idiopathic REM sleep behavior disorder is substantial, with the majority of patients developing Parkinson disease and Lewy body dementia." In this case, you could come up with the following two AIDA sentences: AIDA sentence 1: The risk of developing neurodegenerative disease in idiopathic REM sleep behavior disorder is substantial. AIDA sentence 2: The majority of patients with idiopathic REM sleep behavior disorder who develop a neurodegenerative disease develop Parkinson disease and Lewy body dementia. Three notes on this example: Note 1: The phrase "Although we have found a slightly lower risk than other reports" cannot be represented as an AIDA sentence without more context, and should therefore be dropped. Note 2: The original sentence contains two thoughts and needs therefore be broken down into two atomic AIDA sentences. Note 3: The content of the phrase "developing neurodegenerative disease in idiopathic REM sleep behavior disorder" needs to be repeated in the two AIDA sentences to make them independent from each other.
